School Fish Pods: Food, Learning, and Resilience

Case Study: School Fish Pod Project

A local primary school requested a compact, low-maintenance fish pod to provide fresh protein for meals, teach aquaculture, and act as a living classroom.
Goals:

  • Supplement school meals with fresh fish
  • Teach pond ecology, water science, and business skills
  • Drought resilience, safety, and simple management

Design Options

  • Earthen lined pond — Best for biodiversity and low cost; needs more space
  • Lined pond (HDPE or clay) — Good for small sites; controls water quality
  • Above-ground tank — Portable, classroom-friendly; higher cost per litre

Recommended Example

  • Rectangular lined pond: 4.0 × 2.5 × 1.0 m (~10,000 L water)
  • Low-intensity: 5–10 fish (education), Semi-intensive: 20–30 fish (regular meals)
  • Species: Tilapia (recommended), Catfish (optional), Ornamental koi (demonstration)

Operation & Monitoring

  • Daily: Feed, check fish, log data
  • Weekly: Test water, inspect aeration, clean debris
  • Monthly: Weigh sample fish, partial water change, clean screens
  • Yearly: Desludge, inspect liner, deep clean

Educational Integration

  • Science: water chemistry, ecosystems
  • Math: pond volume, feed calculation
  • Agriculture/Business: cost-benefit, marketing
  • Life Skills: teamwork, responsibility

Students can join a “Fish Pod Club” and rotate as caretakers and data reporters.

Communication & Framing

  • For Administrators: “Supplies X meals, reduces costs, quick to build, safe and easy to manage.”
  • For Teachers/Students: “Hands-on learning: water tests, growth charts, cook a student-grown meal.”
  • For Parents/Community: “Builds food security, teaches life skills, keeps money local.”
  • For Funders: “Scalable, climate-resilient, measurable impact for nutrition and education.”
