Aquaculture — Cold-Climate Tilapia in Kavutiri

Dams, water pans, hatcheries, on‑farm feed and hands‑on training for schools, youth and retirees.

What we do

We combine low-cost civil works (small dams and water pans), practical hatchery techniques, and land-based management ideas inspired by Nordic land‑based aquaculture to grow healthy tilapia through the cool seasons in Embu County.

Pond, Dam & Water‑pan Construction

Earthen ponds, lined water pans, and micro‑dams sized to local rainfall and catchment. Designs include low‑maintenance overflow, sediment traps and secure fingerling intake points.

  • Site survey & slope stabilisation
  • Earthen dam building & compaction guidance
  • Liner options (HDPE), inlet/outlet design
  • Rainwater harvesting & catchment management

Hatchery & Genetics

We source hardier tilapia strains and use seasonal broodstock management, hatchery protocols and simple selective breeding to improve cold‑season survival and early growth.

  • Broodstock selection & conditioning
  • Nursery trays, hapa management and grading
  • Simple selective breeding for local hardiness
  • Biosecurity and disease prevention

On‑Farm Feed & Micro‑Mill

Pelleted feeds tuned for tilapia in cooler water — energy and digestible protein for steady winter growth, made from local ingredients where possible.

  • Feed formulation templates (fingerling → grower)
  • Small extruder/hammermill integration for villages
  • Storage, rationing and feeding schedules in cool water
  • Record keeping & feed conversion tracking

Cold‑Weather Management

Practical measures to protect tilapia when temperatures drop: flow management, sheltering, partial greenhouses for broodstock, and where possible, small land‑based recirculating units for critical stages.

  • Thermal refuges and insulated broodstock tanks
  • Solar or biomass heating for nursery houses (small scale)
  • Reduced feeding strategies and metabolism-aware rations
  • Use of land‑based recirculating modules (RAS) for fingerlings

Training & Community Programmes

Practical, hands‑on training tailored to secondary school students, young farmers, women’s groups and retirees — from hatchery basics to business planning and cooperative marketing.

  • Short courses: Hatchery → Nursery → Grow‑out → Processing
  • School clubs, youth internships and intergenerational mentorship
  • Farmer field schools and demonstration ponds
  • Market access coaching and value‑chain linkages

Inspired techniques from Nordic land‑based systems

We borrow proven ideas from Norway and Iceland — such as modular land‑based systems, controlled flow and robust biofiltration — but refactor them into low‑energy, community‑appropriate modules that work with local power and water availability.

Modular Land‑Based Units

Small RAS-style modules for critical life stages (broodstock, fry) reduce dependency on ambient temperature and provide reliable production windows during the cool months.

Why this works for Kavutiri

  • Combines low‑cost earthworks (dams & water pans) with targeted technology where it matters (nursery/RAS)
  • Local feed production reduces costs and increases food security
  • Training programmes create a pipeline of skilled youth and provide livelihood for retirees
